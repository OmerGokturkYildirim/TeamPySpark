About the Project

Powerful Loans, Extraordinary Impact Data Analysis for Kiva

Microlending has been a game changing way to address international poverty, social equity, and lack of educational parity. Kiva, started in 2005 by a young couple, launched a new strategy to effect social impact through microlending: focus on the progress of the people rather than the situational poverty. This landmark mindshift catalyzed a social movement where all people, everywhere are empowered to give opportunities to the world's most oppressed individuals at an unimaginable scale -- providing loans of more than $1 Billion dollars to date in 99 countries.


About Kiva

  • International, non-profit, crowd funding initiative that facilitates loans via the internet to low-income entrepreneurs and students.
  • Funds student tuition, entrepreneurial ventures, overhead or expansion costs for farmers and small business owners and emergency care.
  • Achievements includes:
    • over 1.9 million lenders from 77 different countries,
    • loans in excess of 1.5 billion dollars(USD) disbursed to 3.6 million borrowers, and
    • Kiva loans have a repayment rate of 95.6%.


How does kiva work?

  1. A borrower applies for a loan.
  2. The loan goes through the underwriting and approval process.
  3. If the loan is approved, it is posted to Kiva for lenders to support.
  4. In the fundraising period, lenders crowd-fund the loan in increments of $25 or more.
  5. When the fundraising is complete, the borrower uses that money according to his/her needs.
  6. Then borrower repays the loan to the lender(s).
  7. The lender(s) use the returned funds to generate new loans, donate, or withdraw the money from the Kiva bank of funds.

What drives a Kiva loan? Factors in the Loan Equation

Team PySpark strove to inform the question: "Which of the variables tracked in the Kiva loan process is most influential in successful loan fulfillment?" Analysis of the Kiva lending framework led to many questions about the data that required additional research and exploration -- and interesting insights.

Funding Time
This field was generated by subtracting RAISED_TIME which is time needed to raise the money for a borrower and POSTED_TIME which is when the loan was made available. This elapsed time was the time needed in days to fund a loan and present to a borrower.

Borrower Genders
The Kiva loans were not always taken up by a single person most of the time it was different groups of male and female recipients. This column has many unique values however, there were about 1,344,252 single female borrowers and 392,282 single male borrowers in the entire dataset that we worked with.

Locations
Kiva loans are available to people globally and there was many country and location data that was used to create the map visualizations featured in this website.

Tags
This column lists hashtags linked with the user input and common words used to explain lender and borrower experience. This field was used to generate word cloud visualizations which highlighted the reoccurring tags.
