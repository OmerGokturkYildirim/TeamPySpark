About the Project

Powerful Loans, Extraordinary Impact Data Analysis for Kiva

Microlending has been a game changing way to address international poverty, social equity, and lack of educational parity. Kiva, started in 2005 by a young couple, launched a new strategy to effect social impact through microlending: focus on the progress of the people rather than the situational poverty. This landmark mindshift catalyzed a social movement where all people, everywhere are empowered to give opportunities to the world's most oppressed individuals at an unimaginable scale -- providing loans of more than $1 Billion dollars to date in 99 countries.

What drives a Kiva loan? Factors in the Loan Equation

Team PySpark strove to inform the question: "Which of the variables tracked in the Kiva loan process is most influential in successful loan fulfillment?" Analysis of the Kiva lending framework led to many questions about the data that required additional research and exploration -- and intersting insights.

Funding Time
This field was generated by subtracting RAISED_TIME which is time needed to raise the money for a borrower and POSTED_TIME which is when the loan was made available. This elapsed time was the time needed in days to fund a loan and present to a borrower.

Borrower Genders
The Kiva loans were not always taken up by a single person most of the time it was different groups of male and female recipients. This column has many unique values however, there were about 1,344,252 single female borrowers and 392,282 single male borrowers in the entire dataset that we worked with.

Locations
Kiva loans are available to people globally and there was many country and location data that was used to create the map visualizations features in this website.

Tags
This column lists hashtags linked with the user input and common words used to explain lender and borrower experience. This field was used to generate word cloud visualizations which highlighted the reoccurring tags.

Interactive Data Map Funding by Country


Kiva funds loans internationally, some areas of the world receive more funding than others. The map itself led to these insights about the data:

  • Three countries received approximately $100 million dollars. Far more than any other country. These three countries include: Peru, Kenya and the Phillipines.
  • There was no funding at all on the Australian continent and very little in the Eurasian area.
  • The United States received quite a bit of funding. Microloans often carry a connotation of serving extremely impoverished communities, the finding that the US received so much funding was surprising.
